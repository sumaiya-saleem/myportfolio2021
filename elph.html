Overview

The Peel Halton Workforce Development Group (PHWDG) is a community based, non-profit corporation. During my internship, my role was to conduct research considering different stakeholders, and design and develop an interactive website on Experiential Learning (EL) on the company's new project on EL in the Peel Halton region, Project ELPH.

Project Goals

  • Educate the different stakeholders (employers, students, parents, educators) on what experiential learning is, providing experiential learning opportunities for High School and University/College students, and the co-benefits of engaging for students and employers
  • Provide informative resources that can help employers connect with different educational institutions across Ontario who are in need of emlpoyers willing to onboard internship and co-op students to partake in experiential learning opportunities.

    • Website Mandate and Rationale

    Before beginning design and research, we presented the website mandate, goals, and low fidelity information architecture and designs to an advisory group consisting of representatives from Peel and Dufferin-Peel School Boards, City of Mississauga and Brampton, University of Toronto, Sheridan College, Walmart Canada, and other employer representatives. The website goals included:

  • Communicate the importance and benefits of experiential learning to employers, students and faculty in academic institutions.
  • Facilitate efficient planning and delivery of experiential learning programs and initiatives for all stakeholders involved.
  • Help grow and support enduring mutually-beneficial relationships built through the experiential learning platform.

    • Information Architecture

    After the advisory committee meeting, we were advised to keep the website more employer based, and a simple navigation to get to there they are looking for with ease.

    Research and Process

    I conducted primary and secondary research on labour trends, government funded employment services, experiential learning theories and best practices, and on other key topics. I identified the four key stakeholders and conducted user research on each of them through surveys. My team and I conducted a meeting with an advisory committee consisting of each of the stakeholders to understand what they would like to learn, and the best way to communicate that information.
